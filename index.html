ABOUT

The High School Artificial Intelligence Entrepreneurship Contest is an interdisciplinary innovation competition for high school students aged 14–18. Centred on artificial intelligence as its core driver, the contest guides students to conduct comprehensive exploration across three dimensions: Intelligence (Technology), Innovation (Business) and Impact (Social Value). Participating teams must address real-world industry problems or societal challenges by leveraging AI technology to build a verifiable minimum viable product (MVP), business model and social impact plan.

The competition adheres to rigorous fairness mechanisms (blind judging, plagiarism compliance checks and outlier-adjusted scoring) and features professional evaluation by a panel comprising university professors, industry engineers and investment advisors. OTLF encourages students to integrate scientific inquiry, entrepreneurial practice and ethical awareness, showcasing their profound insights into future technology and society through original projects.

This competition welcomes students from all backgrounds. We encourage collaboration among peers with diverse skills—whether in technical development, business strategy, design innovation or social research—to jointly complete an innovative project demonstrating AI feasibility, commercial potential and social significance.

Intelligence

Developing foundational capabilities for the AI era. Students must understand AI model principles, data sources, privacy protection, technical limitations and practical application logic.
This dimension emphasizes:
  • Identifying and deploying appropriate AI technologies (e.g., large language models, computer vision, recommendation systems)
  • Matching technical solutions to real-world problem scenarios
  • Data legitimacy, usability, and potential biases
  • Scientific assessment of model limitations, feasibility, and risks
The goal is to cultivate students who can genuinely understand AI, apply AI and evaluate AI, equipping them with core technological literacy for the interdisciplinary era.

Innovation

Encourage students to explore the value logic, user pain points and market opportunities of AI products based on real-world needs.
This dimension focuses on:
  • Needs insight and market research
  • Business model design and long-term viability
  • Minimum viable product (MVP) development, hypothesis testing, and data validation
  • Team collaboration, product thinking, and iterative capabilities
Students will experience the entire journey from idea to verifiable product within a complete business innovation process, cultivating key competencies essential for future entrepreneurship and management.

Impact

Emphasizing responsible AI innovation to ensure technological advancements deliver genuine societal benefits.
This dimension focuses on:
  • Fairness, privacy protection, and identifying and mitigating bias risks
  • Contributions to vulnerable groups, educational equity, health, public services, and related domains
  • Potential societal consequences arising from AI system usage
  • Environmental impact and sustainability (e.g., model efficiency, resource consumption)
The goal is to cultivate the next generation of young innovators who can balance social ethics and sustainable development in technological and business decisions.

Registration

Who Can Register

  • Students aged 14–18 currently enrolled in high school (including IB, AP, OSSD, A-Level, and local curricula)
  • Teams of 5–8 students working on one AI-driven product, service, or business concept

Team Composition

Each team should reflect the 3I structure (Intelligence × Innovation × Impact) with complementary roles, for example:

  • AI / Technical Lead : models, data, system architecture
  • Business / Market Lead : user research, business model, finance
  • Impact / Ethics Lead : social value, ethical AI, ESG & risk thinking
  • Design / Communication Lead : UX/UI, branding, storytelling, pitch

Teams may assign multiple roles to one person, but all three dimensions (technology, business, impact) must be clearly covered.
